> **Primary audience**: Modernization leads, business analysts, engineers
> **Positioning**: The understanding layer across AI and people — one place for humans and AI to share consistent, accurate context for modernization
> **Approved by**: PMM (author), CMO (reviewer, 3 iterations)
> **Date**: 2026-02-24

---

## Strategic Context

### Page Purpose
This product page sits one level deeper than the homepage. The homepage answers "What is this and is it for me?" The product page answers "Show me exactly what I get and how it works." The visitor arriving here has cleared initial awareness — they're evaluating.

### Audience Mental Model
A modernization lead, business analyst, or engineer evaluating Swimm for a legacy/mainframe modernization program. They want to understand: (1) what the platform produces for their team, (2) how AI tools consume the same understanding for forward engineering, (3) whether the outputs are trustworthy and governable, and (4) how it fits their enterprise environment. They're skeptical of marketing — they want to see the product and understand the architecture.

### Core Positioning
Swimm is one place where humans and AI share accurate, consistent understanding of how legacy applications behave. Humans navigate, verify, and govern. AI agents consume and execute. Both operate from the same trusted source. This is the platform that enables the future of engineering — where human governance and AI execution share the same foundation.

### Feature Scope Strategy
Lead with the full platform as a unified story, organized around the 4 conceptual layers from the marketecture:
1. **Code in** (source layer) — what it takes as input
2. **Understanding Engine** (core) — how it works
3. **Understand capabilities** (App Map, BRE, Flows, Glossary, Collections) — what you get
4. **Consume & Act** (Web App + Swimm Assistant, IDE, MCP) — how humans and AI use it

The marketecture visual anchors the hero and tells the architectural story visually.

### Narrative Principles
1. **Show, then explain.** Capabilities first, architecture second. The visitor sees what Swimm produces before learning how.
2. **AI-forward positioning.** Context for AI is a major pillar — potentially equal to or ahead of human consumption in the narrative. The "one understanding layer for humans and AI" framing is the strategic differentiation.
3. **Human governance pairs with AI execution.** Every AI capability section is immediately followed by governance and control. This is responsible modernization, not black-box automation.

## Section-by-Section Brief

### Section 1: Hero + Marketecture (~30 words)

**Job**: Position Swimm as the understanding layer across AI and people. The marketecture visual does heavy lifting — showing the full stack from code input through Understanding Engine to capabilities to delivery (humans and AI). The headline anchors the "one place" positioning.

**Key message**: One place where humans and AI share accurate, consistent understanding of how your applications behave. The understanding layer for modernization.

**Mental state**: "This is a platform for understanding legacy applications — for my team and for AI tools."

**Headline direction for Writer**: Frame around the unified platform concept. The marketecture visual shows the architecture; the headline names what it produces and who it serves. The understanding layer is the concept. Humans navigate it. AI consumes it. Both rely on the same trusted source. Don't fight the visual for attention — let the marketecture do the structural storytelling while the headline captures the positioning.

**What to avoid**: Feature-first framing. Vague "platform" claims. AI-only framing that loses the human element. Repeating homepage hero verbatim.

**CTA**: "See it on your code" or "Request a demo"

**Visual note**: Marketecture SVG — the layered architecture from code input through Understanding Engine to capabilities to delivery (Web App, IDE, MCP). This visual IS the hero. It communicates the full platform architecture in 2 seconds.

---

### Section 2: Application Map (~80 words)

**Job**: Show the primary way humans experience Swimm — the web application where modernization leads, business analysts, and engineers navigate their legacy applications. The Application Map organizes millions of lines of code into browsable business domains, making the entire application navigable without needing the original engineers. Swimm Assistant (natural language Q&A) is part of this experience — anyone on the team can ask questions about the codebase.

**Key message**: Your application, organized into browsable business domains. Modernization leads see the full scope. Business analysts navigate to the processes and business rules that matter. Engineers drill from domain to process to step to source code. Every level includes AI-generated summaries, dependency graphs, and business context. And anyone on the team can ask questions in natural language via Swimm Assistant.

**Mental state**: "I can actually navigate this application. My whole team can — not just the engineers who built it."

**What to avoid**: Dev-centric language. Framing this as an "engineering tool." Missing the business analyst angle — this is where BAs see business rules in context. Missing Swimm Assistant as the accessible entry point for non-technical team members.

**Visual note**: Product screenshot — Application Map domain view showing business-named domains with process counts and drill-down navigation.

---

### Section 3: Business Rules & Flows (~100 words)

**Job**: Show the core analytical capabilities that modernization programs depend on. Business rule extraction and flow/dependency analysis carry 60% of POC evaluation weight — these are what buyers measure Swimm against. Frame for the modernization use case: these produce the understanding that modernization teams need to make confident decisions.

**Key message**: Business rules extracted automatically in plain English, categorized (Input, Calculation, Invoking, Output), and traceable to source code. Execution paths traced from entry to exit across every call chain and dependency — at unlimited depth. Both presented in business language. Business analysts see rules in terms they understand. Engineers see the complete technical picture. Everyone works from the same source of truth.

**Mental state**: "This extracts the business logic we need for modernization — in language the whole team can work with."

**What to avoid**: Dev-only framing. Generic "extracts rules" without depth. Ignoring the "unlimited depth" differentiator for flows. Losing the "same source of truth for everyone" thread.

**Visual note**: Product screenshots — (a) business rules table with categories, (b) flow graph view or code walkthrough with interleaved business-language prose and source code.

---

### Section 4: Glossary & Collections (~80 words)

**Job**: Show the knowledge layer that bridges code-derived understanding and organizational knowledge. The Glossary translates cryptic code names into the organization's business language and feeds into every other feature. Collections package understanding into goal-oriented deliverables that SMEs edit and enrich. This is where automated understanding meets human governance — the first touch of the control narrative that pairs with the AI section that follows.

**Key message**: Cryptic variable names become business terms automatically. The Glossary feeds into every feature — flows, business rules, and walkthroughs all use your organization's language. Collections package understanding into editable deliverables where subject matter experts annotate, correct, and add context that only exists in people's heads. Code-derived knowledge meets organizational knowledge.

**Mental state**: "The business side can actually work with this. And our SMEs can enrich it with what they know."

**Writer note**: This section bridges from "what the platform produces for humans" to the AI consumption story that follows. The editability and SME enrichment here foreshadow the governance narrative. Make the transition feel natural — what SMEs verify and govern becomes the trusted context AI consumes.

**What to avoid**: Positioning Collections as documentation. Underplaying editability. Losing the audience after the high-impact BRE+Flows section.

**Visual note**: Collections view — rich wiki-style content with Mermaid diagrams and editable sections.

---

### Section 5: Context for AI (~120 words)

**Job**: This is a major positioning section — potentially the most strategically important section on the page. Swimm's understanding layer isn't just for humans browsing the web app — it's the trusted context that AI coding assistants, agents, and modernization platforms consume for forward engineering. This is the "enabling the future of engineering" narrative. The same verified understanding that modernization leads work with becomes the context AI agents use for code generation, migration, and transformation.

**Key message**: AI coding assistants and agents access the same verified understanding your team relies on — via MCP. Forward engineering grounded in accurate application behavior, not hallucination. The same business rules, flows, dependencies, and glossary terms that your modernization leads work with become the context AI agents use for code generation, migration, and transformation. One understanding layer. Consistent context for humans and AI. This is how the future of engineering works.

**Mental state**: "This is how we bridge human understanding and AI execution. The same source of truth for both."

**Narrative weight**: This is not a feature section — it's a positioning section. The message is strategic: Swimm is the platform where human governance and AI execution share the same foundation. The future of engineering runs on verified understanding, not inference.

**Proof points**: 75% average response time savings, 61% average cost savings from efficient token utilization (Claude Code benchmark). Self-service MCP setup — generate an API key, connect any AI tool.

**What to avoid**: Positioning Swimm as an AI tool. Treating MCP as a checkbox feature. Missing the strategic narrative: this is about enabling the future of engineering. Burying the Swimm Assistant's role as the bridge between human Q&A and AI-consumed context.

**Visual note**: MCP configuration view or Swimm Assistant chat interface showing grounded answers. Concrete, not abstract.

### Section 6: Human Governance & Control (~100 words)

**Job**: The essential pair to Section 5. The power of AI consuming verified understanding only works if humans govern that understanding. This section establishes the control mechanisms: SME verification, editability, freshness tracking, traceability, deterministic analysis. The message: humans stay in control of what AI consumes.

**Key message**: Every insight traces to source code. Freshness indicators show whether analysis matches the current code version. SMEs verify, enrich, and approve what the platform produces. Collections are editable — your team controls the knowledge that AI tools consume. Deterministic analysis means no inference, no hallucination in the underlying understanding. Human governance, AI execution.

**Mental state**: "We control the understanding. AI consumes what we've verified. That's the right architecture."

**Narrative pairing**: Sections 5 + 6 form a unit. Section 5: AI tools consume verified understanding (the opportunity). Section 6: Humans govern that understanding (the control). Together they tell the story of responsible, effective modernization with AI.

**What to avoid**: Defensive tone about AI risks. Abstract "trust" language without specific mechanisms. Missing the "human governance, AI execution" framing that ties both sections together.

---

### Section 7: How It Works (~80 words)

**Job**: The engine behind everything — for visitors who want to understand the technical approach. Now that they've seen capabilities (Sections 2-4), the AI story (Section 5), and governance (Section 6), the architecture explanation confirms the mechanism.

**Key message**: Deterministic static analysis parses uncompiled source code and builds a complete structural graph — call chains, dependencies, data transformations — at unlimited depth. Plugin-based: 21+ languages with mainframe-first focus. AI translates deterministic findings into business language via the customer's own LLM. No compilation needed. No mainframe access needed.

**Mental state**: "Now I understand the mechanism. This is why the outputs are trustworthy."

**What to avoid**: Leading with mechanism (it follows the story). Overcomplicating the architecture. Making the AI layer sound like the source of truth.

**Visual note**: Simplified pipeline diagram: Code in → Static Analysis → Structural Graph → AI Translation → Business Language Outputs.

---

### Section 8: Enterprise Readiness (~60 words)

**Job**: Qualification checklist for the modernization program's security and infrastructure requirements.

**Key message**: Fully on-premise — no code leaves your network. Your LLM (Azure OpenAI, OCI, Bedrock). SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certified. 21+ languages with mainframe-first coverage (COBOL, PL/I, JCL, Assembler, RPG + Java, C/C++, Python, and more). Plugin-based extensibility for customer-specific parsing. IDE plugins for VS Code, Visual Studio, and JetBrains with bidirectional code linking.

**Mental state**: "This works in our environment."

**What to avoid**: Marketing-level security claims. Missing the "customer's own LLM" point. Generic enterprise language.

**Visual note**: Icon grid/checklist. Language logos. Certification badges.

---

### Section 9: Conversion (~30 words)

**Job**: Final CTA. Reassert the unified platform value — one understanding layer for the team and for AI.

**Key message**: One understanding layer for your modernization team and your AI tools. See it on your code.

**CTA**: "See Swimm on your code" (filled button) + friction reducer ("Free evaluation on your codebase")

**Mental state**: "I want to see this on our applications."

**What to avoid**: Multiple CTAs. New information. Friction. New messaging.

## Key PMM Decisions

1. **Audience**: Modernization leads, business analysts, engineers — not dev/architect-centric
2. **Core positioning**: One place for humans and AI to share consistent, accurate context for modernization
3. **AI-forward**: Context for AI is a major positioning pillar (Section 5), paired with human governance (Section 6)
4. **Marketecture in hero**: Visual architecture tells the platform story; headline anchors the positioning
5. **No "gap" section**: Technical audience knows the problem — go straight from hero to capabilities
6. **Web UI primary**: Application Map and web experience lead the capabilities story; IDE is a line item in Enterprise Readiness
7. **Feature scope**: Full platform, organized by marketecture layers (not feature list)
8. **Section ordering**: Show capabilities → AI story → governance → mechanism → enterprise → convert
9. **Swimm Assistant**: Placed in Section 2 (web experience) as the accessible entry point for the whole team
10. **IDE**: Brief mention in Section 8 (Enterprise Readiness) — not a dedicated section
