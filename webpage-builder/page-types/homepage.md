# Homepage — Page Type Template

Section structure, copy rules, and visual guidelines for building a homepage.

## Sections

A homepage typically includes 8-10 sections in this sequence. Not all are required — choose based on what the company has to show. Sections marked (optional) can be omitted or merged.

| # | Section | Job | Visitor's Question |
|---|---------|-----|--------------------|
| 1 | **Hero** | What is this, is it for me, what do I do next | "What is this?" |
| 2 | **Value proposition** | Expand the hero's promise into the buyer's world | "What does that mean for me?" |
| 3 | **Capabilities / completeness** | Make the value concrete — what do I actually get | "What do I actually get?" |
| 4 | **Differentiation** | Why should I trust this approach over alternatives | "Why should I trust this?" |
| 5 | **Credibility / proof** | Transfer authority via logos, quotes, numbers | "Who else trusts this?" |
| 6 | **Process / how it works** (optional) | Show the path from complexity to clarity | "How does it work?" |
| 7 | **AI / platform layer** (optional) | Forward-looking strategic position | "Where is this going?" |
| 8 | **Enterprise readiness** | Qualification checklist — security, scale, deployment | "Can this work in my environment?" |
| 9 | **External validation** (optional) | Third-party analyst recognition | "Does the market recognize this?" |
| 10 | **Conversion** | Final CTA — reassert value, reduce friction | "What do I do next?" |

### Section Details

**Hero** (~30 words)
- Headline (8-12 words) + subheadline (15-25 words) + CTA button. No body paragraph.
- Anchor the product positioning and mirror the buyer's tension, then resolve it.
- Primary CTA (request demo / see product in action).
- Visual: brand-level graphic or animation. Not a product screenshot.

**Value proposition** (~80 words)
- Section headline + 3-4 short value blocks (headline + one supporting sentence each).
- Frame around buyer outcomes, not features or technology.
- Visual: iconography or simple illustrated concepts. No product screenshots yet.

**Capabilities / completeness** (~120 words)
- Section headline + 4-6 capability cards (label + one supporting sentence).
- Demonstrate scope and substance. Make the business dimension explicit.
- Visual: first section where product screenshots earn their place. Show 2-3 real views.

**Differentiation** (~80 words)
- Section headline + 3 differentiation points (bold claim + supporting sentence).
- Establish why this approach is fundamentally different and more reliable.
- Visual: traceability graphic, before/after contrast, or conceptual comparison.

**Credibility / proof** (~40 words of own copy)
- Logo bar + 1 testimonial + 2-3 proof point numbers. Mostly visual.
- Mid-page CTA after social proof (trust peaks here).
- Visual: grayscale logo bar, testimonial card, large-format numbers.

**Process / how it works** (~80 words, optional)
- 3-5 phase cards showing the path from input to outcome.
- Architecture diagram if appropriate.
- Visual: SVG diagram at desktop, card stack at mobile.

**AI / platform layer** (~100 words, optional)
- Position the product as a foundational layer consumed by both people and AI tools.
- Visual: architecture reference diagram (not marketing infographic).

**Enterprise readiness** (~60 words)
- 4-6 checklist items: security, deployment, scale, coverage.
- Visual: icon grid, certification badges, language/platform logo strip.

**External validation** (~30 words, optional)
- Analyst badge + framing sentence. Can merge with credibility if thin.

**Conversion** (~30 words)
- Headline restating core value + CTA button + friction reducer line.
- No imagery. CTA button is the single dominant visual element.

**Total homepage copy target: ~550-600 words.** Visuals carry equal or greater weight than text.

## Copy Rules

### Outside-in messaging
Every sentence starts from the buyer's world, not the product. Lead with what changes for the buyer. Name the real tension before resolving it. Never assume the visitor cares yet — earn it every section.

### Punchy structure
- **Headlines carry the story**: Reading only headlines should tell the full value proposition.
- **One idea per block**: If you need a second point, start a new block.
- **Short by default**: 2-3 sentences per block maximum.
- **No throat-clearing**: First sentence of every section does real work.

### Sentence-level discipline
- **Active voice, strong verbs**: "[Company Name] reconstructs application behavior" not "Application behavior is reconstructed."
- **Rhythm over uniformity**: Short declaration + substantive supporting sentence. Avoid three or more consecutive short sentences.
- **No filler**: Zero instances of "very," "really," "simply," "just," "actually," "in order to."
- **Numbers over adjectives**: "90% faster" not "dramatically faster."

### Tone guardrails
- **Confident and direct, not casual or clever**: No performative energy, no exclamation marks.
- **No marketing-speak**: No "unlock," "empower," "transform," "revolutionize," "cutting-edge," "next-generation," "seamless," "end-to-end solution."
- **No fear tactics**: State real urgency factually, don't weaponize it.
- **BBQ test, boardroom edit**: Draft as if explaining to a smart peer, then edit for the precision a boardroom demands.

## Visual Guidelines

### Page rhythm
Alternate dark and light section backgrounds to create scroll cadence:
- Dark sections: Hero, Capabilities, AI/Process, Conversion
- Light sections: Value, Differentiation, Enterprise readiness
- Light-gray: Credibility, Validation

### Typography framework

| Element | Desktop | Tablet | Mobile |
|---------|---------|--------|--------|
| H1 (Hero headline) | 48-56px, weight 700 | 44px | 36px |
| H2 (Section headlines) | 36-40px, weight 700 | 34px | 30px |
| H3 (Block headlines) | 20-24px, weight 600 | 20px | 18px |
| Body | 15-16px, weight 400 | 15px | 15px |
| CTA button text | 16-18px, weight 600 | 16px | 16px |
| Small/labels | 12-14px, weight 400-500 | 12px | 12px |

### Color discipline
- Colors must be justified by zone and purpose — restraint over decoration.
- Limit accent colors to 1-2 per page. Earn each one.
- Dark backgrounds: use muted blues and grays for body text, white for headlines.
- Light backgrounds: use dark charcoal for headlines, muted dark for body text.

### CTA moments
Three CTA placements per homepage:
1. **Hero** (primary): Filled button, brand color
2. **Mid-page** (after credibility): Ghost/outlined button
3. **Final** (conversion): Filled button, slightly larger than hero CTA

### Responsive approach
- Mobile-first with tablet and desktop breakpoints
- Desktop: multi-column grids, side-by-side layouts
- Tablet (768-1024px): reduced columns, scaled typography
- Mobile (<768px): single-column stacking, full-width CTAs

### Section padding
- Standard vertical padding: 80-96px at desktop, 48-64px at mobile
- Content max-width: 1200px centered
