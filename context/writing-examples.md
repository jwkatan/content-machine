## Why This File Matters

Writing examples are the single most important input for voice-matching. The AI agents analyze these examples to learn:

- **Sentence rhythm**: How you mix short and long sentences
- **Vocabulary choices**: Which words you prefer and avoid
- **Hook patterns**: How you open articles to capture attention
- **Explanation style**: How you break down complex topics
- **Transition flow**: How you connect sections and ideas
- **Tone calibration**: The specific register between casual and formal

Without real examples, the agents default to generic B2B writing. With good examples, they produce content that sounds like your team wrote it.

---

## What to Include

Paste **3-5 of your best-performing published articles** below. Choose articles that:

1. **Performed well**: High traffic, engagement, or conversions
2. **Sound like you**: These represent the voice you want replicated
3. **Cover different formats**: Mix how-to guides, thought leadership, and product content
4. **Are recent**: Reflect your current voice, not how you wrote two years ago
5. **Are complete**: Include the full article, not just excerpts

### What makes a good example

- An article your team is proud of
- Content that received positive reader feedback
- Posts that rank well in search (signals relevance and quality)
- Articles that generated leads or meaningful engagement

### What to avoid

- AI-generated articles (the agent learns patterns it already knows - no value added)
- Articles from other companies (voice mismatch)
- Heavily edited pieces that don't reflect natural voice (ghostwritten content is fine if it represents target voice)
- Outdated content that no longer reflects your standards

## After Adding Examples

Once you've pasted your articles, review them against your `brand-voice.md` to make sure they genuinely represent the voice you want. If an article has sections that don't match your target voice, note that in the "What Makes It Great" section so agents know which parts to emphasize and which to de-prioritize.

**Recommended**: 5 examples for strong voice matching
